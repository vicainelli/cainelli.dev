PayU taught me an important lesson early: in payments, product decisions show up in revenue almost immediately.

I spent most of my time on checkout optimization. We ran A/B tests on form layout, copy, and interaction flow, then measured changes carefully before moving on. Through a mix of UX and performance improvements, we increased conversion by 30%.

That work forced discipline. Every field and every millisecond had to justify itself. I learned how to run cleaner experiments and avoid changing too many variables at once.

I also led the institutional site rebrand, coordinating product and marketing requirements and turning them into a coherent experience.

My years at PayU shaped how I still work today: care about speed, reliability, and measurable outcomes, not just implementation details.
