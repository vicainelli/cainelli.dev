I joined IKEA to work on the IKEA Family rewards program. It sounds simple from the outside, but running it across 20+ countries means different languages, regulations, and local expectations.

My focus has been the frontend platform that markets build on top of. A lot of the work is standardization: shared patterns, safer defaults, and fewer one-off implementations that become expensive later.

One project I am especially proud of was rebuilding our CI/CD setup. We had conflicting pipelines and slow feedback cycles, so I moved us to GitHub Actions on Google Cloud Platform and managed the infrastructure with Terraform. Build and test time dropped by about 60%, and the setup later became a reference for other teams.

I also put testing at the center of delivery. We improved coverage with Jest, React Testing Library, and Playwright, then enforced quality gates in CI. The practical result was fewer production incidents and more confidence to ship.

Security and team effectiveness are still a big part of my day to day: dependency hygiene, vulnerability remediation, mentoring, and cross-team coordination. Not glamorous work, but it keeps the product stable and teams moving.
