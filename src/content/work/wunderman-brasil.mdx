Wunderman was my first real tech lead role. I moved from shipping my own tickets to making decisions that affected entire teams.

Agency life is a different game. Every client has different constraints, timelines, and priorities. I defined architectures and migration plans that were flexible enough for that reality while still keeping codebases maintainable.

Migration work was a big part of my role. Most projects could not afford a full rewrite, so we modernized incrementally and kept systems stable during the transition.

I also spent a lot of time between design and engineering, setting delivery standards and reducing avoidable friction. Better collaboration mattered as much as technical direction.

This was also the period when Wunderman and J. Walter Thompson merged into Wunderman Thompson, so change was constant. It taught me to stay calm in ambiguity and keep teams focused on what had to ship next.
