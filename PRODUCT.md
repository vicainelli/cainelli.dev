# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two audiences with equal weight:

1. **Recruiters and hiring managers** evaluating fit for senior/staff frontend roles. They scan for signal: can this person ship quality UI, think about product, and communicate clearly?
2. **Peer engineers and designers** interested in frontend craft, design systems, and product thinking. They look for depth: real work, genuine opinions, things worth reading.

Both arrive via a link (LinkedIn, Bluesky, a job posting, a shared article). They decide in seconds whether to stay.

## Product Purpose

cainelli.dev is a personal portfolio and writing surface for Vinicius Cainelli, a Staff Product Engineer focused on frontend and UI. It exists to:

- Showcase work history and product ownership (IKEA Family, JustEat Takeaway, VIVO)
- Share thinking through writing (TIL, building-in-public, longer pieces)
- Express personality beyond a resume — concerts, music, the things that make the person
- Convert visitors into conversations (job opportunities, collaborations, peer connections)

Success means a visitor leaves thinking: "this person has taste, ships quality work, and I'd enjoy working with them."

## Brand Personality

**Friendly, expressive, clear.**

- Warm but not corporate. Personality-forward without being loud.
- Technical credibility through craft, not jargon.
- Monospace as identity (IBM Plex Mono) — developer-native, but not cold.
- Generous spacing and clear hierarchy (ref: daniloleal.co).
- The site should feel like talking to a sharp, approachable engineer over coffee.

Voice: first person, conversational, direct. No filler, no buzzwords.

## Anti-references

- **Generic AI-generated portfolios**: cream/beige backgrounds, gradient text, eyebrow+kicker patterns, identical card grids. If it looks like every AI output of 2025-2026, it's wrong.
- **Corporate LinkedIn energy**: stock-photo vibes, buzzword-heavy, template layouts, safe-to-the-point-of-bland.
- **Over-designed SaaS landing pages**: glassmorphism, hero metrics, feature grids, dark-mode-by-default-for-coolness. This is a person, not a product launch.

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity over cleverness.** Every element should earn its place. If it's decorative without function, remove it.
2. **Personality through craft, not decoration.** Expressiveness comes from spacing, typography, color choices, and micro-interactions — not from adding more stuff.
3. **Monospace is identity.** IBM Plex Mono carries the technical character. Lean into it rather than fighting it with mixed fonts.
4. **Generous space, clear hierarchy.** Content needs room to breathe. Typographic scale should guide the eye without shouting.
5. **Show the person, not just the engineer.** Concerts, music, building-in-public — these aren't filler. They're the signal that makes the portfolio memorable.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA compliance: semantic HTML, keyboard navigation, sufficient color contrast (4.5:1 body text, 3:1 large text).
- Light/dark theme support (already implemented) with system preference detection.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for any animations.
- Alt text on images, aria labels on interactive elements.
